Our Services

Discover exceptional dental care in a comfortable and modern setting.

Whether you're here for a regular check-up or a specialized procedure,

we're dedicated to helping you achieve a healthy, confident smile.

Check-up

Regular dental check-ups are essential for maintaining a healthy smile. During your visit, our dentist will thoroughly examine your teeth, gums, and mouth to detect any signs of cavities, gum disease, or other oral health issues. We also provide professional cleaning to remove plaque and tartar buildup that brushing alone can't reach. Early detection and prevention help avoid more serious and costly treatments in the future. At A.N.G Dental Care Center, we make every check-up comfortable and informative, ensuring your oral health is always in good hands.


During a dental consultation, the dentist may:


  • - Examine your teeth, gums, and mouth
  • - Take X-rays if needed
  • - Identify any dental issues (like cavities or gum disease)
  • - Recommend treatments or procedures
  • - Answer your questions about dental care or cosmetic options

Cleaning

Oral prophylaxis, commonly known as professional teeth cleaning, is a preventive dental procedure that removes plaque, tartar, and stains from the teeth. It helps prevent tooth decay, gum disease, and bad breath. During the procedure, a dental professional uses specialized tools to clean above and below the gum line, followed by polishing with a special paste. Regular oral prophylaxis is essential for maintaining optimal oral hygiene and a healthy, confident smile.


During an oral prophylaxis procedure, the dentist may:


  • - Remove plaque and tartar from teeth surfaces
  • - Clean above and below the gum line
  • - Polish your teeth with a special paste
  • - Apply fluoride treatment if needed
  • - Advise on daily brushing and flossing techniques
  • - Help prevent gum disease, tooth decay, and bad breath

Jacket Crown

A jacket crown is a type of full-coverage dental crown that encases the entire visible portion of a tooth. It is commonly made from porcelain or ceramic and is designed to restore the shape, strength, and appearance of a damaged, decayed, or discolored toothâ€”especially front teeth where aesthetics matter most.


During a jacket crown procedure, the dentist may:


  • - Examine and prepare the affected tooth
  • - Remove decayed or damaged portions
  • - Shape the tooth to fit the crown properly
  • - Take impressions for a custom-made crown
  • - Place a temporary crown while the permanent one is made
  • - Cement the final crown in place for a natural look and strong protection

Root Canal Treatment

Root canal treatment is a dental procedure used to save a severely decayed or infected tooth by removing the damaged pulp inside. The space is then cleaned, disinfected, and sealed to prevent further infection. This treatment relieves pain and allows the tooth to function normally without needing extraction.


During a root canal procedure, the dentist may:


  • - Examine the tooth and take X-rays to assess the damage
  • - Numb the area with local anesthesia
  • - Create an opening to access the pulp chamber
  • - Remove infected or damaged pulp tissue
  • - Clean and disinfect the inside of the tooth
  • - Place a crown or filling to restore tooth strength and appearancel

Tooth extraction

Tooth extraction is the removal of a tooth from its socket in the jawbone. It is typically done when a tooth is severely damaged, decayed, infected, or causing crowding. Extractions can be simple or surgical, depending on the condition of the tooth and surrounding tissues.


During a tooth extraction procedure, the dentist may:


  • - Examine the tooth and take X-rays if needed
  • - Numb the area with local anesthesia
  • - Loosen the tooth using specialized instruments
  • - Gently remove the tooth from the socket
  • - Place gauze to control bleeding and help with clot formation
  • - Provide aftercare instructions for healing and pain management

Tooth restoration

Tooth restoration refers to the repair or replacement of a damaged, decayed, or missing part of a tooth to restore its function, shape, and appearance. Common types include fillings, crowns, inlays, onlays, and composite bonding.


During a tooth restoration procedure, the dentist may:


  • - Examine the tooth and assess the extent of damage
  • - Remove decayed or damaged portions of the tooth
  • - Clean and prepare the area for restoration
  • - Choose the appropriate material (composite, amalgam, ceramic, etc.)
  • - Shape and place the restorative material
  • - Polish and adjust the restoration for a natural look and comfortable bite

Dentures

Dentures are removable appliances used to replace missing teeth and surrounding tissues. They help restore the ability to chew, speak, and smile with confidence. Dentures can be full (replacing all teeth) or partial (replacing only a few missing teeth), and are custom-made for a natural and comfortable fit.


During the denture process, the dentist may:


  • - Examine your mouth and assess missing teeth or gum condition
  • - Take impressions of your mouth to create a custom fit
  • - Measure your bite and alignment for proper jaw function
  • - Create trial dentures and make necessary adjustments
  • - Deliver the final dentures and ensure a comfortable fit
  • - Provide guidance on cleaning, maintenance, and wear

Braces

Orthodontic treatment is used to correct misaligned teeth and jaws, improving both appearance and function. It involves the use of braces, aligners, or other dental appliances to gradually move teeth into proper alignment. Orthodontics not only enhances your smile but also helps prevent oral health issues caused by improper bite or crowded teeth.


During an orthodontic treatment process, the dentist may:


  • - Examine your teeth, bite, and jaw alignment
  • - Take X-rays, photographs, and dental impressions
  • - Develop a customized treatment plan
  • - Place braces, clear aligners, or other appliances
  • - Schedule regular adjustments or check-ups
  • - Monitor progress and make changes as needed
  • - Provide retainers after treatment to maintain results

Teeth Whitening

Teeth whitening is a cosmetic dental procedure that lightens the color of teeth by removing stains and discoloration. It helps enhance the appearance of your smile and is safe and effective when performed under professional supervision. Whitening can be done in-office or through take-home kits provided by your dentist.


During a teeth whitening procedure, the dentist may:


  • - Examine your teeth to ensure you're a good candidate
  • - Clean your teeth to remove surface debris
  • - Apply a protective barrier to your gums
  • - Apply a professional-grade whitening gel to the teeth
  • - Use a special light or laser to activate the gel (in-office treatment)
  • - Provide post-treatment care instructions to maintain results
  • - Offer take-home whitening trays if needed for follow-up
